About DIPCA - Dow International Patient Care Association.

DIPCA, or Dow International Patient Care Association, was founded in 2012 and is a resident non-profit, non-political welfare organization that aims to bridge the gap between needy patients and healthcare.


Mission Statement: Dow International Patient Care association is the resident non-profit, non-political welfare organization that aims to bridge the gap between needy patients and healthcare.


Since it's conception in 2012, countless lab tests, surgeries and treatments have been provided by the student-run organization. Funding is obtained through generous donations and events held on the Dow University campus.


Transparency has always been maintaibed by a designated treasurer and reciepts, as well as records are maintained for each patient served by DIPCA.


Alongside a daily shift in the hospital for needy patients, DIPCA's medical students hold events to serve the community. These included Ramadan food packages and a medical camp at an orphanage last year.


There are also a plethora of benefits for students who are members of this organization. From an early stage, they are exposed to doctor-patient interaction and develop crucial professional medical history skills in shifts. Volunteers are also given the opportunity to learn the basics of hospital and lab operation through this experience when referring patients to the Dow lab. Lastly, they meet fellow givers and undoubtedly develop teamwork skills when organizing or attending Dipca events.
